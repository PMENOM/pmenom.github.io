  • [Hochschild homology of reductive p-adic groups note], 2025.3.

    This is the lecture talk given by Maarten Solleveld from Radboud Universiteit Nijmegen. The goal of this mini-course is to explain a description of the Hochschild homology of the Hecke algebra of a reductive p-adic group, in spectral terms related to the Bernstein center. Lecture 1: Introduction to Hochschild homology and twisted crossed product algebras. Lecture 2: Representation theory and Hochschild homology of graded Hecke algebras. Lecture 3: Hochschild homology of algebras associated to p-adic groups. Details can be found in the 'Hecke algebras of p-adic groups' and 'Homology of Hecke algebras' parts at his homepage. Tbh I didn't catch the main idea.

    In p-adic day of Tempered representations and C* algebra, he gave a talk on [Topological K-Theory of reductive p-adic groups], which described how the K-theory can be computed from the space of irreducible tempered-representations. The final description is closely related to conjectures by Aubert-Baum-Plymen-Solleveld.

  • [Berstein Center] , 2025.3.

    In this series of lectures, we will explain J. Bernstein's description of the center of the category of smooth representations of reductive p-adic groups as rational functions on the variety of irreducible representations. The main reference is his book.
