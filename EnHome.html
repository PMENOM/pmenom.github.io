About me

I am currently (i.e. 2024 Fall) an graduate student at Università di Padova (Italy, M1), next year I will continue my master degree at Universität Duisburg-Essen (Germany, M2). I'm participating in the program called ALGANT. Information for ALGANT master's students at Padova is HERE, and for Essen is HERE.

I got my undergraduate degree in M.Sc. at the School of Mathematics, Renmin University of China, where I was supervised by Prof. Shanwen Wang. I was also a visiting research student in BICMR, Peking University, and graduated from the program Enhanced Program for Graduate Study there. It was where I learnt real mathematics.

My research area is number theory, in particular the representation theory and automorphic forms.

I am also interested in Algebraic Geometry, including Complex Geometry and Arithmetic Geometry, and Harmonic analysis. These ‘hobbies’ serve me some estimation techniques from analysis (eg. Langlands classification) and the geometric realization of the abstract objects (eg. Borel-Weil theorem).

Interest: piano, classical music (favourite: Bach, Beethoven, Bruckner, Wagner), swimming (national 2nd-class), badminton, football (favourite team: Arsenal, Argentina, Barça), coffee.

Notes

  • [Personal Note on the Parameters and Theta Lifts (Sp-O)] , 2024.10.

    In this paper, we study, following Paul’s work on the Howe correspondence for symplectic-orthogonal dual pairs, the Harish-Chandra paramters and the Langlands-Vogan paramters of both sides, and translate the statement in Paul's paper to the Langlands-Vogan parameter version. The result is more convenient to relate the local root numbers, which is used by Gan–Gross–Prasad to state the precise local Gan–Gross–Prasad conjecture in [GGP12].

    The main part of the theory is written by first two authors. My note is bascically want to fullfill all the details and explain the theories as explicitly as I can.

    Since the author did not decide whether to add any parts of this note into the formal paper, all contents of this paper is for reference and study purposes only, please do not spread temporarily.

  • [Local Gan-Gross-Prasad Conjecture: Tempered Hermitian Case] , 2024.4.

    In this paper, we study, following work of Beuzart-Plessis in [BP20], a geometric expansion for certain multiplicities m(\pi) appearing in the local Gan-Gross-Prasad Conjecture, and Beuzart-Plessis’s proof on a weak form of this conjecture (on the multiplicity one property in tempered L-packets). In Beuzart-Plessis’s proof, the crucial step is to study a sort of local relative trace formula which is related to the Gan-Gross-Prasad Conjecture for unitary groups over a local field F of characteristic zero.

    It is also the english version of my bachelor thesis.

  • [Notes on Perfectoid Spaces (updating)] , 2023.2-8.

    This is the note for the graduate course in 2023 Spring semester: Number Theory: Perfectoid Spaces. Last November, Prof. Yiwen Ding asked me to give him a talk on some topics related to the perfectoid spaces. So I added some pages about Fargues-Fontaine Curve in this note.

  • [notes on modular forms and automorphic forms] , 2023.3.

    This note answers the question``How does the classical theory of modular forms connect with the theory of automorphic forms on GL_2?'' and aims to use the representation theory to study them. At last we give some results about Whittaker models and the L-functions.

  • [Notes on Classification with example on GL(2)], [The Second Part], 2023.8.

    This is the handwritten note on the representation seminar in 2023 summer. It answers the Local Langlands Correspondence for GL_1 and GL_2, in both archimedean and non-archimedean place. Knowledge on the Galois side is new for us. One Interlude here: Borel Weil Thm (and Beilinson-Bernstein Localization). Then we studied the Langlands Classification and Discrete Series on Arichimedean places with many examples, especially on SL_2(R)\cong SU(1,1), Sp(4,R), and SU(2,2). We gave a specific calculation on the leading term and the global character for some representations, and explained the idea behind the proof of Langlands Classification.

    Notes are here: [Discrete Series, Langlands Classification, with examples], the [second], [third], [fourth] part.
